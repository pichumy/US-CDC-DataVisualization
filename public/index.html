Crude Mortality Rates in the United States 1999 - 2016

Information taken from Centers for Disease Control and Protection

The Crude Rate is the total number of deaths to residents in a specified geographic area (country, state, county, etc.) divided by the total population for the same geographic area (for a specified time period, usually a calendar year) and multiplied by 100,000.

Darker areas correspond to areas where a larger percentage of deaths occur in comparison to how many people live in the area
